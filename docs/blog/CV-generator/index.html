The 4-Step Process

Instead of wrestling with formatting quirks and text boxes in standard editors, we can outsource the heavy lifting to an AI model (like ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini) and execute the final design in Overleaf:

  1. Let AI Handle the Logic: Paste your raw work history, education, and skills into your AI of choice. Prompt it to: "Act as an expert resume writer. Structure my provided history into a clean CV, outputting the final result strictly as LaTeX code using a modern, minimalist template."
  2. Bring it into Overleaf: Copy the generated code, navigate to Overleaf.com, and create a free account. Open a "Blank Project" and paste the raw code directly into the editor.
  3. Tweak and Customize: Rather than fighting a drag-and-drop UI, you maintain total control. Browse Overleaf's massive library of free, professional templates or simply ask the AI to tweak the margins and fonts for you.
  4. Export a Flawless PDF: Hit the "Recompile" button and download your document. You now possess a pristine, high-resolution PDF—no watermarks, no paywalls, and no recurring subscriptions.

What is Overleaf

Overleaf acts much like Google Docs, but it is specifically built to compile LaTeX:

  • A Code-Based Approach: LaTeX (pronounced LAY-tech) is a high-quality document preparation system heavily utilized in academia and STEM fields. You use plain text and code tags to tell the document exactly how to look. (aka never hassle with page numbers again in word!)
  • Live Cloud Preview: The platform provides a cloud-based editor on the left side of your screen and a live, updating PDF preview on the right, keeping your workflow seamless and visually clear.
